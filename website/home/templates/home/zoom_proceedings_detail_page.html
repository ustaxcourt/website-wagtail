What should I do the day of my Zoomgov proceeding?

Sign in early, check your internet connection, ensure your display name is accurate, and mute yourself unless speaking.

Who can be in the Zoomgov courtroom during a proceeding?

Participants may include judges, court personnel, attorneys, and other required personnel.

What happens when I join the proceeding?

You may enter a virtual waiting room. Follow court instructions when admitted.

Do I have to be on video?

Video is typically required, but certain situations allow for exceptions.

Will my telephone number be visible if I call in?

No, phone numbers are masked for privacy.

Does the Court have any tips for participating in a Zoomgov proceeding?

Use a quiet location, ensure good lighting, and speak clearly.

What if I get disconnected?

Reconnect using the same meeting link or call the court for assistance.

Can I record the Zoomgov proceeding?

No, recording is strictly prohibited.

What if I want to speak with my representative or obtain privacy?

The judge may allow private breakout rooms.

What is a breakout room?

Breakout rooms allow private discussions.

Will the Court allow low-income taxpayers and cultural oral programs to participate?

Yes, special accommodations may be provided.
